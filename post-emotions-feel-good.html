Emotions feel good.

Published on January 15, 2025 • 3 min read
Philosophy Emotion

We were made to feel. Not just to think, not just to act, but to feel. And when we feel, we connect. When we connect, we create. When we create, we live.

The Power of Emotional Resonance

Think about the last time something truly moved you. Maybe it was a piece of music that sent chills down your spine. Or a story that made you cry. Or a moment of pure joy that made your heart swell.

That feeling—that's not just a chemical reaction in your brain. That's you, fully alive, fully human, fully present in the moment.

"Emotions are not the opposite of reason. They are the foundation of it."

Why We Resist Feeling

In our modern world, we've been taught to suppress our emotions. To be "professional." To be "rational." To be "logical." But here's the truth: we're not robots. We're human beings with hearts that beat and souls that feel.

The Myth of Objectivity

We like to think we're objective, that we make decisions based purely on facts and logic. But every decision we make is colored by our emotions, our experiences, our hopes, and our fears.

And that's not a weakness—it's a strength. Our emotions are our compass, guiding us toward what matters most.

Emotions in Design

As a software engineer, I've learned that the best products aren't just functional—they're emotional. They make people feel something.

Creating Emotional Connections

When I design a user interface, I don't just think about usability. I think about how it will make someone feel. Will it make them feel confident? Excited? Calm? Inspired?

  • Color: Warm colors create comfort, cool colors create calm
  • Typography: Serif fonts feel trustworthy, sans-serif feels modern
  • Spacing: Generous whitespace creates breathing room
  • Animation: Smooth transitions create delight

The Science of Feeling

Our emotions are incredibly sophisticated. They're not random—they're the result of millions of years of evolution, helping us survive and thrive.

Emotional Intelligence

Emotional intelligence isn't about being emotional. It's about understanding emotions—ours and others'. It's about using that understanding to make better decisions and build better relationships.

In software development, this means:

  • Understanding how users feel when they use our products
  • Creating experiences that reduce frustration and increase joy
  • Building teams where people feel safe to express themselves
  • Designing systems that respect human emotions

Embracing Vulnerability

One of the most powerful things we can do is embrace our vulnerability. To admit that we don't have all the answers. To ask for help when we need it. To share our fears and our hopes.

In the tech world, we often try to appear invincible. But the truth is, we're all human. We all make mistakes. We all have doubts. We all need support.

"Vulnerability is not weakness. It's the birthplace of innovation, creativity, and change."

What Makes Things Resonate

So what makes something truly resonate with us? What makes it feel good?

Authenticity

When something is authentic, when it's real and honest, we can feel it. We're drawn to it because it speaks to our own truth.

Connection

We're social creatures. We crave connection. When something helps us connect with others or with ourselves, it feels good.

Growth

We're wired to grow, to learn, to evolve. When something helps us become better versions of ourselves, it resonates deeply.

Beauty

Beauty isn't just visual. It's in the elegance of a well-designed system, the clarity of a well-written piece of code, the harmony of a well-orchestrated team.

Creating with Heart

As engineers, we have the power to create things that make people feel. Not just things that work, but things that work beautifully. Things that bring joy, that solve problems, that make life better.

When we create with heart, when we design with emotion, when we build with feeling, we create something that lasts. Something that matters. Something that makes a difference.

Conclusion

Emotions feel good because they're what make us human. They're what connect us to each other and to the world around us. They're what drive us to create, to innovate, to make things better.

So let's not suppress our emotions. Let's embrace them. Let's use them to create things that matter. Let's build with heart, design with feeling, and code with soul.

Because when we do, we don't just build better software—we build a better world.

"The best code is written with heart, the best designs are created with feeling, and the best products are built with love."
